# Weekly Review

For the week of {{date:YYYY-MM-DD}}.

## Closing Reflection

{{One sentence: what mattered most, what changed, and what next week needs.}}

## Look Back

### Outcomes

{{What actually happened that mattered? Include shipped work, relationship/life wins, decisions, avoided mistakes, and concrete evidence. This is not a task list.}}

### Learning / Patterns

{{What did the week teach you? What changed your model of your work, yourself, relationships, health, systems, or priorities? Prefer "this keeps happening because…" over isolated anecdotes.}}

### Friction / Capacity

{{What made the week harder? Include blockers, overcommitment, health, energy, emotional drag, calendar mismatch, unclear scope, or recovery debt. Name causes without blame.}}

## Get Current

### Current Reality

{{What do your task board, calendar, journals, and other source systems say is actually true now? Synthesize attention, WIP, stale items, blocked themes, and capacity. Do not duplicate task management.}}

### Open Loops / Risks

{{What loose ends, commitments, risks, or under-visible issues need a decision, slot, deferral, delegation, or deletion? Keep this to the few that matter.}}

## Look Ahead

### 3–5 Outcomes

{{Choose outcomes, not a task pile. Each should be important enough to protect time for.}}

### Boundaries / Tradeoffs

{{What should not happen next week? What good things are competing? What are you consciously choosing, deferring, or refusing?}}

### Experiments / Feedback Loops

{{What small test, customer/advisor question, behavior change, or operating experiment would reduce uncertainty next week? For anything you want adopted: "If I improved one thing this week, what would most increase your willingness to use it or recommend it to someone else?"}}

## Blind Spot Check

{{Top 1–3 material blind spots only. Force the sharp versions: what opportunity am I missing, what should I stop doing, and where am I lying to myself? Each answer should include evidence and a next action. Empty is acceptable if nothing material appears.}}
